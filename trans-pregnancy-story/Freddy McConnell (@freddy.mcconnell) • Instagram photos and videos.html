    It feels wild to be focusing on introducing myself today, given the state of the world…

    Yet I’m here, you’re here and I am making the positive choice to share this pregnancy… I’m already 16 weeks and, surreal as it feels, life marches on…

    So, that’s me 👆soon after I was born in 1986. I’m smiling at my mum at home in SE London. The two of us have remained about this close ever since. ❤️

    We moved to the Kent coast 2 yrs later when my sister came along… I’m 9/10yo at Dreamland, Margate. That’s my mum and stepdad who I was just getting to know at the time. He’s my third and equally-beloved parent. ❤️

    The 3rd pic is me around 12/13 and my baby bro, Oscar. It’s partly thru helping care for him that I realise my affinity for kids and strong desire to be a parent myself.

    School and uni are miserable. I cope by travelling to far off places, like Ramallah, in 2007. I study Arabic at uni and scrape thru. By final year in 2009, I’m too distracted by the internets helping me realise I’m “transgender”, a word I’m hearing for the first time.

    5. 2015-ish with mum, on a local beach. My first staff job in journalism is at The Guardian. I have my own home. I’m 2-3y on T, 2y post top-surgery in Florida and I’ve never felt so confident and content. All this contributes to my decision to pursue my dream of a family. Solo parenthood feels like a natural fit.

    With support from my GP and endocrinologist, I pause T in late 2016. Early 2017, I conceive on 2nd attempt at IUI with donor sperm. Pregnancy is smooth but dysphoria is all consuming. I doubt myself constantly.

    I keep it all private but plan to share the experience after, once I’m back on T, in a documentary film. That plan becomes @seahorsefilm. 💙💙💙

    SJ (aka Shrimp) is born into the water in hospital in January 2018. It’s a powerful, positive experience. Mum is my birth partner. The NHS midwives who look after us are wonderful.

    In 2020, I feel ready to go again - to complete our little family. It takes longer to conceive but I’m more resilient. And I’m not hiding this time. I’ve learned from other seahorse dads to take more pride in what we can do. No apologising, no justifying.

    And that brings us to now... 🤓✌️
